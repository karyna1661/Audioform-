# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-03-02*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:**
Audioform is the highest-signal feedback layer for builders who ship in public.

**What it does:**
Audioform lets creators publish voice surveys and collect spoken responses that preserve tone, hesitation, conviction, and emotional nuance. It converts scattered public feedback into structured insight loops creators can replay, classify, and act on faster.

**Product category:**
Voice feedback infrastructure for public builders.

**Product type:**
SaaS.

**Business model:**
Early-stage SaaS; beta model pending (likely freemium + paid tiers for advanced analytics/workflows).

## Target Audience
**Target companies:**
Solo builders, indie hacker projects, early-stage startups, Web3/product communities shipping in public.

**Decision-makers:**
Founder-builders, product builders, creator-operators, community-led PMs.

**Primary use case:**
Collect high-conviction feedback on product changes and turn it into fast iteration decisions.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Hear what users actually feel, not just what they type.
- Filter low-effort noise and surface high-signal feedback.
- Compress scattered feedback into actionable product decisions.

**Use cases:**
- Feature launch validation.
- Onboarding friction diagnosis.
- Messaging and value proposition testing.
- Weekly “ship in public” feedback loop.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| Founder-builder | Speed + signal quality | Drowning in shallow text comments | High-conviction voice signal in one place |
| Product operator | Decision clarity | Feedback scattered across channels | Structured, replayable insight by question/theme |
| Community-led creator | Trust + engagement | Performative engagement, low depth | Human-feeling feedback with emotional context |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:**
Public builders get lots of feedback volume but low insight quality.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Form tools optimize for short typed answers.
- Social replies optimize for visibility and performative takes.
- DMs are unstructured and hard to operationalize.

**What it costs them:**
Slower decision cycles, false positives from shallow feedback, iteration fatigue.

**Emotional tension:**
"Are people being polite, or is this real conviction?"

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Typeform, Google Forms, SurveyMonkey - strong for structure, weak on emotional bandwidth and conviction signal.

**Secondary:** X/Farcaster comments, Discord threads, DMs - authentic at times, but scattered and noisy.

**Indirect:** Traditional user interviews - deep but slow and hard to run continuously.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Voice-first response capture.
- Effort-gated feedback quality.
- Conviction and depth-oriented feedback loop.
- Replayable insight artifacts.

**How we do it differently:**
We frame feedback as a recurring decision loop: ask -> collect voice -> classify signal -> iterate.

**Why that's better:**
Voice carries intent and emotion; builders can make faster, higher-confidence product calls.

**Why customers choose us:**
They need quality signal under shipping pressure, not another generic survey UI.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Voice is higher friction than text." | That friction is productive; it filters low-conviction noise and raises signal density. |
| "People won't respond in voice." | Public builders with engaged communities get stronger participation when asks are short and specific (20-45 sec prompts). |
| "I can already use a form tool." | Form tools capture answers; Audioform captures conviction and emotional context for faster decisions. |

**Anti-persona:**
Teams seeking only bulk, low-effort quantitative survey completion with no need for depth.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:**
Scattered, shallow feedback and uncertainty about what users actually feel.

**Pull:**
High-signal voice responses that are structured and reusable for decision-making.

**Habit:**
Defaulting to forms/comments because tools are familiar.

**Anxiety:**
Fear that voice may reduce response volume or feel awkward.
