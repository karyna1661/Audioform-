the# Audioform Marketing Department OS (GTM + Content)

Last updated: 2026-03-27
Primary positioning: Audioform is a machine that turns community emotion into strategic clarity.

## 1) Department Charter

Mission:
Build category leadership for "high-signal feedback infrastructure" and drive repeatable creator activation loops.

North star:
Increase number of public builders who run weekly voice feedback loops and act on those insights.

Department outcomes:
1. Positioning clarity in market language.
2. Predictable content production and distribution.
3. Measurable conversion from audience -> creator -> retained creator.

## 2) Core Narrative Architecture

Category claim:
Voice feedback infrastructure for builders shipping in public.

Enemy:
Shallow, scattered, performative text feedback loops.

Old world:
Comments, forms, DMs, and noise.

New world:
Voice conviction, structured signal, fast decision cycles.

Core message pillars:
1. `Signal over noise`: "Text is cheap. Voice is commitment."
2. `Entropy reduction`: "Feedback is everywhere, insight is nowhere. Audioform compresses chaos into decisions."
3. `Builder speed`: "Ship, collect conviction, iterate."
4. `Proof-of-thought`: "Voice captures conviction, not just opinion."

## 3) Marketing Department Design

## Team Structure (Lean v1)

1. Head of GTM (owner: strategy + revenue narrative)
- Owns positioning, roadmap alignment, launch calendar, KPI governance.

2. Content Lead (owner: editorial + thought leadership)
- Owns content engine, publishing standards, angle rotation, content QA.

3. Distribution Lead (owner: channel ops)
- Owns X/Farcaster distribution, creator partnerships, repurposing cadence.

- Owns attribution, event quality, dashboards, experiment readouts.

5. Product Marketing Manager (owner: messaging + enablement)

Staffing reality for early stage:

## 5) GTM Workflow Orchestration

Workflow A: Narrative -> Content
1. Define one tension statement (e.g., "Public builders are drowning in shallow replies.")
2. Convert into 3 content angles (conviction/filtering/emotional bandwidth).
3. Produce one anchor asset (essay/thread/video).
4. Atomize into 8-12 derivative posts.

Workflow B: Product Shipping -> Proof Distribution
1. Ship product change.
2. Run voice survey with targeted prompt.
3. Extract 1-2 high-signal clips.
4. Publish "what we changed and why" with voice proof.
5. Invite builders to run same loop.

Workflow C: Feedback -> Insight Capital
1. Collect voice responses.
2. Transcribe and tag by theme/question/conviction.
3. Surface strongest decision-relevant clips.
4. Feed insights into product and public narrative.

## 14) Founder Social Content Cadence

When generating or scheduling social content, rotate through these 7 core pillars to build a multifaceted public image:
1. **User behavior:** Tweet about what users do a lot.
2. **Product stage:** Tweet about the product and exactly what stage we are in (e.g., beta, scalability phase).
3. **Engineering stress:** Once in a while, tweet about adding features that will intentionally stress out the engineering team.
4. **Investor updates:** Tweet about what’s going on with investors.
5. **Partnerships:** Tweet about the kind of partners we are looking to work with.
6. **Public company building:** Build the public image about the company and let people know they can ask questions.
7. **Ecosystem involvement:** Get involved in the ecosystem we are building in:
   - Tweet about the cool things technical founders are doing and how you genuinely love it.
   - Tweet about interesting meetings that offer unique insights into the business/field.
   - Tweet about our products and ecosystem—the things we love, the things we find unbelievable.
   - Tweet about non-technical guidance: marketing, distribution, sales.
   - Tweet about personal stuff connected to the business but that doesn't reveal too much.

Farcaster:
We think "low-quality feedback" is often a system design problem, not a user problem.

X:
What we want from feedback:

Farcaster:

Farcaster:

Signal density matters more than raw volume.

Farcaster:

Farcaster:

Farcaster:
