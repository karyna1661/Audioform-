# Audioform Dogfooding Program (Build in Public)

Last updated: 2026-03-02
Goal: Use Audioform internally as the primary feedback system for improving Audioform, and publish the loop publicly.

## 1) Program Objective

Turn product iteration into a visible, repeatable proof loop:
1. Ship change.
2. Ask targeted voice question.
3. Collect conviction-rich responses.
4. Decide and implement next change.
5. Publish what changed and why.

This creates:
- Better product decisions.
- Authentic social proof.
- A model other builders can copy.

## 2) Weekly Dogfooding Loop

Cadence: 1 cycle per week (minimum).

Monday (Prompt Design):
1. Choose one product decision to validate.
2. Create one Audioform survey with 3-5 prompts.
3. Share to private tester group and public build thread.

Tuesday-Wednesday (Collection):
1. Collect at least 10 voice responses.
2. Tag responses by question + depth bucket.
3. Highlight top 3 conviction clips.

Thursday (Decision):
1. Summarize top themes and conflicts.
2. Make one explicit product decision based on responses.
3. Log decision rationale.

Friday (Public Proof):
1. Publish "What we heard / what we changed."
2. Share 1-2 anonymized clips or transcript snippets.
3. Invite builders to run the same loop.

## 3) Standard Survey Templates (Internal)

Template A: Feature Validation
1. "What is the first thing you understood from this flow?"
2. "Where did you hesitate, and why?"
3. "If we change one thing first, what should it be?"

Template B: UX Friction
1. "Describe the exact moment this felt unclear."
2. "What did you expect to happen next?"
3. "How would you rewrite this step in your own words?"

Template C: Messaging Clarity
1. "In your own words, what does Audioform do?"
2. "What part sounds generic or unconvincing?"
3. "What line made you trust this most?"

## 4) Metrics to Track

Operational:
1. Surveys run per week.
2. Responses per survey.
3. Median response duration.
4. % deep responses (>20s).

Decision quality:
1. Product decisions made from dogfooding responses.
2. Time from survey publish to decision.
3. Post-change reduction in friction event.

Marketing proof:
1. Weekly public posts published.
2. Engagement rate on dogfooding case posts.
3. Creator signups attributed to dogfooding posts.

## 5) Public Artifact Format

Use this exact structure weekly:
1. "What we shipped"
2. "What we asked (voice prompts)"
3. "What we heard (themes + clip)"
4. "What we changed"
5. "What we will test next"

## 6) Guardrails

1. Never publish raw user audio without consent.
2. Anonymize clips/transcripts by default.
3. Separate internal tester feedback from customer-critical decisions.
4. Keep one decision focus per weekly survey.

## 7) Roles

1. Product owner: picks decision target and approves changes.
2. Research operator: runs survey + response synthesis.
3. Content/distribution owner: publishes weekly proof post.
4. Growth ops: tracks attribution and loop metrics.

## 8) 4-Week Pilot Plan

Week 1:
- Recorder UX: playback state and clarity.

Week 2:
- Thank-you conversion path to creator signup.

Week 3:
- Dashboard information architecture (survey stack vs moderation queue).

Week 4:
- Survey composer logic (intent mode -> prompt recommendations).

Success criteria after 4 weeks:
1. 4 public dogfooding reports shipped.
2. >= 1 product decision implemented each week from voice feedback.
3. Clear uplift in at least one activation metric.
