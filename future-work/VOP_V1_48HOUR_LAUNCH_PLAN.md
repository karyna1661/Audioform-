## Executive Summary

This is the minimum viable execution plan to validate Audioform's core value proposition: **Better questions → Better voice → Better insights**.

**Goal:** Collect 100-300 voice responses in 48 hours using manual insight extraction. No AI required yet.

**Output:** Simple insight report + 5-10 best insight clips to prove concept and attract partners.

---

## The Big Idea

### One Question, Global Impact

**Question:**  
> "What is the biggest challenge facing your community right now?"

**Why This Question:**
- Universal (applies everywhere)
- Personal (lived experience, not statistics)
- Actionable (reveals problems that can be solved)
- Emotional (triggers stories, not just facts)

**Expected Insights:**
- Healthcare access
- Education barriers
- Unemployment
- Infrastructure issues
- Safety concerns

#### Hour 16-24: Manual Insight Extraction

**Process (Founder-Led):**

1. **Listen to All Responses** (2-3 hours)
   - Take notes while listening
   - Flag powerful moments
   - Note recurring themes

2. **Identify Top Themes** (30 min)
   - Group similar responses
   - Count frequency of each theme
   - Calculate rough percentages

3. **Select Best Clips** (1 hour)
   - Look for:
     - Clear message (easy to understand)
     - Emotional intensity (passion, frustration, hope)
     - Specific details (not vague generalities)
     - Good audio quality
   - Target: 5-10 clips, 15-30 seconds each

4. **Transcribe Key Quotes** (1 hour)
   - Type out exact words from best clips
   - Note speaker context (anonymous demographics)
   - Timestamp location in audio file

## Question Script (Use Exactly As Written)

### Core Questions (Ask All Participants)

**Q1: Context Setting**
> "What's your role in your community? (e.g., student, entrepreneur, parent, teacher, etc.)"

*Purpose:* Provides demographic context without being invasive

---

**Q2: Main Question**
> "What is the biggest challenge facing your community right now?"

*Purpose:* Core insight generator

---

**Q3: Moment Activation**
> "Can you describe a specific moment when you personally encountered this challenge?"

*Purpose:* Triggers story memory, produces richer response

---

**Q4: Emotional Impact**
> "How does this challenge make you feel? What emotions come up when you think about it?"

*Purpose:* Captures emotional truth, not just facts

---

**Q5: Imagination Activation**
> "If you could magically change one thing about this situation, what would it be?"

*Purpose:* Reveals desired outcomes, solutions-oriented thinking

---

### Optional Follow-Up Questions (If Time Permits)

**Q6: Community Impact**
> "Who else is affected by this? How wide is the impact?"

---

**Q7: Previous Solutions**
> "What have people tried so far to address this? Why hasn't it worked yet?"

---

**Q8: Call to Action**
> "If decision-makers heard your voice right now, what would you want them to do?"

## Manual Extraction Guide

### Step 1: Active Listening

**While Listening to Each Response:**
1. Note key themes mentioned
2. Mark timestamp of powerful moments
3. Rate emotional intensity (1-5 scale)
4. Transcribe standout quotes
5. Note audio quality (for clip selection)

**Template:**
```
Response #042
Duration: 0:47
Country: Nigeria (Lagos)
Speaker: Female entrepreneur, ~30s

Themes Mentioned:
- Electricity reliability (primary)
- Business operations impact
- Youth frustration

Powerful Moments:
- 0:12-0:24: "We plan our whole week around which days NEPA brings light"
- 0:31-0:43: "How am I supposed to tell investors 'come invest' when..."

Emotional Intensity: 4/5 (clear frustration, passion)
Audio Quality: Good (clear, minimal background noise)
Clip Candidate: YES (both moments above)
```

---

### Step 2: Theme Identification

**After Listening to All Responses:**

1. **List All Themes Mentioned**
   ```
   - Electricity/power (mentioned 32 times)
   - Healthcare access (24 times)
   - Education barriers (19 times)
   - Unemployment (38 times)
   - Transportation (15 times)
   - Internet connectivity (22 times)
   ```

2. **Group Related Themes**
   ```
   Infrastructure Cluster:
   - Electricity (32)
   - Internet (22)
   - Roads/transport (15)
   
   Social Services Cluster:
   - Healthcare (24)
   - Education (19)
   
   Economic Cluster:
   - Unemployment (38)
   - Cost of living (17)
   ```

3. **Calculate Percentages**
   ```
   Total Responses: 127
   
   Youth Unemployment: 38/127 = 30%
   Power/Electricity: 32/127 = 25%
   Healthcare: 24/127 = 19%
   Education: 19/127 = 15%
   Internet: 22/127 = 17%
   ```

---

### Step 3: Clip Selection Criteria

**Score Each Potential Clip (1-5 Scale):**

| Criterion | Weight | Score | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-------|-------|
| Clarity of Message | 25% | /5 | Easy to understand in one listen |
| Emotional Intensity | 25% | /5 | Passion, frustration, hope audible |
| Relevance to Theme | 20% | /5 | Clearly represents the theme |
| Uniqueness | 15% | /5 | Fresh perspective, not generic |
| Audio Quality | 10% | /5 | Clear, minimal background noise |
| Duration Fitness | 5% | /5 | 10-30 seconds ideal |

**Overall Score Formula:**
```
(Clarity × 0.25) + (Emotion × 0.25) + (Relevance × 0.20) + 
(Uniqueness × 0.15) + (Quality × 0.10) + (Duration × 0.05)
= Overall Score (0-5)
```

**Selection Threshold:**
- 4.0+ : Must include
- 3.5-3.9: Strong candidate
- 3.0-3.4: Backup option
- <3.0: Exclude

---

**Writing Style:**
- Use direct quotes (preserve speaker's exact words)
- Provide context before each clip
- Keep analysis minimal (let voices speak)
- Use bold headers for scannability
- Include visual elements (waveforms, photos if possible)

**Data Handling:**
- Remove identifying information from transcripts
- Blur or modify voices if requested
- Store raw data securely (encrypted)
- Limit access to core team only

**Publication Guidelines:**
- Never reveal full names without explicit consent
- Obscure specific locations if safety concern
- Provide content warnings for sensitive topics
- Allow speakers to review before major publications

Traditional community research relies on surveys that reduce human experiences to checkboxes and statistics. This approach misses crucial context, emotion, and lived reality that drives real understanding.
